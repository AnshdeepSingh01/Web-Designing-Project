Petra, Jordan

About the Wonder

Petra, often referred to as the "Rose City", is one of the most breathtaking and mysterious archaeological sites in the world. Carved directly into the vibrant red and pink sandstone cliffs of southern Jordan, Petra was once a thriving trading hub and the capital of the ancient Nabataean Kingdom. Today, it stands as a testament to human ingenuity, blending natural beauty with architectural brilliance. Founded as early as the 4th century BCE, Petra flourished between 400 BCE and 100 CE, when the Nabataeans established it as a major crossroads of the ancient world. Its strategic location connected Arabia, Egypt, and the Mediterranean, making Petra an important stop for traders dealing in spices, silk, and incense. The Nabataeans were masterful engineers. They developed a sophisticated water collection and distribution system that allowed them to thrive in the arid desert climate. Petra's decline began after a series of earthquakes and the shifting of trade routes, and by the 7th century, it was largely abandoned. Western explorers rediscovered Petra in 1812, and it has since captured the imagination of archaeologists, historians, and travelers worldwide.

Best Time to Visit

Visit in spring (March to May) or autumn (September to November) for pleasant weather.
